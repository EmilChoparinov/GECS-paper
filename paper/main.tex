% \begin{abstract}
%   This paper introduces a graph based approach to designing Concurrent ECS Architectures. Entity Component Systems, also widely known as ECS's, are one of the most important architectures used in real-time simulations. Through decoupling data from logic, ECS's excel at facilitating compositions of dynamic objects. The design introduced in this paper is wait-free and as such, the design does not force synchronization through locks and prefers caches and transactions instead. There are three parts to this thesis. Chapter \ref{chap:1} covers existing ECS literature including the motivation as to why to use ECS over Object Oriented Programming (OOP) in real-time simulation contexts. Chapter \ref{chap:2} introduces a formal view of ECS systems developed for the graph based approach in Chapter \ref{chap:3}. With this formalization, the graph model represents entities within a runtime finite-state-machine. Chapter \ref{chap:3} introduces the GECS C library constructed for this paper.
% \end{abstract}
